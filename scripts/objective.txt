1. Stochastic Calculus & SDE FoundationsWhile Mathlib recently added basic probability theory, Markov kernels, and martingales, the actual machinery to solve SDEs is missing.
Missing - Itô Calculus: Lean currently lacks a robust, generalized formalization of Itô's Lemma. You cannot prove SDE stability without this.
Missing - Stochastic Integrals: The construction of the Itô integral (and Stratonovich integral) for continuous semi-martingales is highly incomplete.
The Goal for your AI: Have your AI prove basic properties of Brownian motion (Wiener processes) or formalize the Picard-Lindelöf theorem for the existence and uniqueness of solutions to basic 1D SDEs.

2. Advanced Partial Differential Equations
Mathlib has basic topology, measure theory, and some 1D calculus, but it hits a wall when dealing with modern, multidimensional fluid dynamics.
Missing - Advanced Sobolev Spaces: While basic $W^{1,2}$ spaces exist, fractional Sobolev spaces and strict embedding theorems (like the Rellich-Kondrachov theorem in arbitrary dimensions) are full of holes.
Missing - Theory of Distributions: To do PDEs, you need weak derivatives. The Schwartz space of test functions is there, but the full, rigorous algebraic manipulation of distributions for non-linear PDEs is lacking.
The Goal for your AI: Point your AI at formalizing Leray-Hopf weak solutions. This is the absolute prerequisite before anyone can even begin to formulate the Navier-Stokes Millennium problem in Lean.

3. Differential Geometry and Complex AnalysisIf you want to move away from applied physics and into pure math, this is the biggest gap in the library right now.
Missing - Complex Manifolds: Mathlib has topological spaces and some basic real manifolds, but complex manifolds (and Kähler geometry) are essentially nonexistent.
Missing - Geometric Analysis: The mathematical machinery required to formally state Ricci Flow (the tool Perelman used to solve the Poincaré Conjecture) is missing.
The Goal for your AI: Use your pipeline to formalize the Frobenius Theorem or the Poincare Lemma for differential forms.

4. Statistical Learning Theory (SLT)While a massive update just dropped in February 2026 adding Dudley's entropy integral and basic Gaussian processes, deep learning theory is still a barren wasteland in Lean.
Missing - Neural Tangent Kernels (NTK): There is no formal proof in Lean that infinitely wide neural networks converge to Gaussian processes.
Missing - Advanced Convergence: Formal proofs for the convergence of modern optimization algorithms (like Adam) in non-convex landscapes.The Strategy for your PipelineDo not ask your MCTS to invent new math. Ask it to translate textbook math into Lean 4. 
You take a standard graduate textbook on Stochastic Calculus, extract a known, human-proven lemma (e.g., "The quadratic variation of standard Brownian motion is $t$"), define the types manually, and let your URM loop figure out the exact sequence of Lean tactics to make the compiler accept it.
